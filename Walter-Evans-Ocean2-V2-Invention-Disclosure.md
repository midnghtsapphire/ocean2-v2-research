**1. ABSTRACT**

The Ocean-2 V2 is an improved wave energy converter (WEC) designed for deep-ocean deployment. It utilizes a novel "bobbing ball" mechanism integrated with an overtopping power cycle to harvest kinetic energy from ocean waves. Unlike traditional shore-limited systems, the Ocean-2 V2 is optimized for high-energy deep-sea environments. The system features a modular design constructed from rust-proof alloys and composites, rated for a maximum power capacity of 500-600 KW. A key innovation in V2 is the integration of onboard green hydrogen conversion, allowing for the storage and transport of energy without the need for expensive subsea cabling.

**2. BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION**

Traditional renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind, are often restricted to coastal areas due to space constraints and the high cost of energy transmission. Wave energy represents a vast, untapped resource, but existing converters face challenges related to survivability in harsh marine conditions and the "energy transfer gap"—the difficulty of transporting electricity from deep-ocean sites to the mainland. The original Ocean-2 prototype (V1) successfully demonstrated the overtopping power cycle in Puget Sound, achieving a 50 KW output. However, V2 seeks to address the scalability and transmission limitations of the original design.

**3. SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION**

The Ocean-2 V2 introduces several key improvements over the V1 design:
*   **Bobbing Ball Mechanism:** A spherical buoyant body that undergoes controlled vertical displacement (heaving) in response to incident waves.
*   **Internal Hydro-Turbines:** As the sphere bobs, water is forced up a submerged tube and into the sphere, where it drains through internal hydro-turbines to generate electricity.
*   **Integrated Hydrogen Conversion:** Onboard electrolysis units convert generated electricity into green hydrogen, utilizing seawater as the feedstock.
*   **Modular Scalability:** The 10-meter wide units are designed for deployment in large-scale arrays, aiming for city-scale power generation by 2030.

**4. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS**

*   **Rated Power Capacity:** 500 KW (Nominal), 600 KW (Maximum).
*   **Dimensions:** Approximately 10 meters in width.
*   **Materials:** High-strength, rust-proof alloys and advanced marine composites.
*   **Mechanism Type:** Hybrid Overtopping / Point Absorber (Bobbing Ball).
*   **Conversion System:** Onboard seawater electrolysis for Green Hydrogen (H2) production.
*   **Control System:** Integrated Oceans 2.0 API for real-time monitoring and analytic alerts.

**5. PRIOR ART ANALYSIS**

*   **Point Absorbers (e.g., WO1999022137A1):** Existing point absorbers use heaving buoys but often lack the integrated overtopping drainage system of the Ocean-2.
*   **Overtopping Devices:** Traditional overtopping systems are typically large, shore-bound structures. The Ocean-2 V2 miniaturizes and modularizes this process for deep-sea use.
*   **Hydrogen Integration (e.g., Huertas-Fernández et al., 2021):** While theoretical models for OWC-hydrogen systems exist, the Ocean-2 V2 provides a specific mechanical implementation using the bobbing ball overtopping cycle.

**6. CLAIMS STRUCTURE (PROVISIONAL)**

1.  A wave energy converter comprising a buoyant spherical body and a submerged vertical tube, wherein wave action induces vertical displacement of the body.
2.  The converter of claim 1, further comprising internal hydro-turbines configured to capture energy from water overtopping into the spherical body through the submerged tube.
3.  The converter of claim 1, further comprising an onboard electrolysis system configured to convert electricity generated by the turbines into green hydrogen.
4.  The converter of claim 1, constructed from modular rust-proof alloys and composites for deep-ocean survivability.
