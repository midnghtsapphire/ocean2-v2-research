### The Mechanical Blueprint
- **The Overtopping Power Cycle**: Waves force water up a submerged tube into a sphere where it drains through hydro turbines
- **Resilient Modular Construction**: Built at 10 meters wide using rust-proof alloys and composites to survive relentless sea conditions
- **500 KW Rated Power Capacity**: High-capacity, clean energy generation in deep open oceans

### Ocean-2 Prototype Performance Metrics
- Maximum Rated Power: 500 KW
- Prototype Test Output: 50 KW (Puget Sound)
- Prototype Power: 50 KW (Promssa)
- Device Width: ~10 Meters

### Roadmap to Blue Economy Innovation
- **Step 1: Solve the Energy Transfer Gap** — Onboard Green Hydrogen: Design inventions that convert electricity into green hydrogen onboard to bypass expensive mainland cabling
- **Step 2: Build Apps on Digital Infrastructure** — Utilize Oceans 2.0 API data streams to create real-time monitoring and alert-derived analytic tools
- **Step 3: Prioritize Ecosystem Sustainability** — Ensure designs are uninvasive and use satellite tracking to monitor impact on marine biodiversity

## ocean2_infographic.jpg — "Ocean-2: Tapping the Blue Ocean for Clean Energy"
- Developed by Panthalassa
- Harvesting deep-ocean wave power for scalable, sustainable energy production

### The Problem & Innovation
- Shore-Limited Renewables: Traditional renewables restricted to coastal areas, facing scalability and space constraints
- **The Solution: Ocean-2 Wave Energy Converter**
- **The "Bobbing Ball" Mechanism**: Waves cause the sphere to bob, forcing water through internal turbines to generate electricity

### Specs
- Maximum Capacity: 500 KW (Prototype rated for 600 kW maximum power capacity)
- Flexible Modular Design: Built from rust-proof alloys and composites to withstand high-energy seas

### The Opportunity: Scaling for the Future
- **Deep Ocean Deployment**: Affordable deployment in deep open oceans, unlike shore-bound systems
- **Minimal Ecological Impact**: Uninvasive, quiet design developed with biologists to ensure safety for marine ecosystems
- **Hydrogen Conversion Potential**: Future iterations may use power to split seawater into exportable green hydrogen (Seawater to Green Hydrogen)

### Ocean-2 Development Progress
- Prototype (Puget Sound): 50 KW Output — Demonstrated survivability in tricky currents
- Rated Capacity (TRL 7): 500 KW Max
- 2030 Vision: Large-Scale Arrays — Aiming to power entire coastal cities
