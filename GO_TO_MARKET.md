# Go-To-Market (S2M) — Ocean2 V2 Wave Energy

## 1) Problem and thesis
Deep-ocean wave resources are energy-dense, but commercialization is constrained by reliability, marine O&M, and expensive export infrastructure. Ocean2 V2 is positioned around modular deployment plus an energy-transfer strategy (including hydrogen pathways) to reduce cable dependency risk.

## 2) Market context (2025–2026 research snapshot)
- Wave energy remains pre-commercial but continues to scale through pilot deployments and demonstration arrays.
- Cost ranges in recent public analyses vary widely by design maturity and site, with demonstration-stage LCOE commonly above mature renewables but improving with scale, reliability gains, and O&M improvements.
- Public programs (DOE WPTO, IEA-OES member initiatives, EU/UK marine energy programs) continue to fund pilot-to-precommercial transitions.

## 3) Commercial wedge strategy
1. **Phase A — Technical credibility:** survivability and uptime validation in harsh-sea conditions.
2. **Phase B — Contracted pilots:** utility, island-grid, defense, and offshore industrial microgrid use cases.
3. **Phase C — Array scaling:** replicate modular units into bankable multi-device projects.

## 4) Buyer segments
- Coastal utilities with resilience mandates
- Island / remote communities facing high imported-fuel costs
- Ports and maritime logistics hubs
- Offshore operators requiring local low-carbon power

## 5) Competition and alternatives
- Competes with offshore wind, floating solar, batteries, and diesel replacement systems in remote sites.
- Differentiation must be proven in availability, survivability, total installed cost, and integration flexibility.

## 6) Revenue model options
- Device sales + deployment services
- Long-term O&M contracts
- IP licensing for converter design / controls
- Energy or hydrogen offtake partnership structures

## 7) Risks and mitigations
- **Technical risk:** Storm survivability → staged ocean testing and design-for-maintenance.
- **Financing risk:** High first-of-a-kind capex → blended capital (grants + strategic partners + pilot PPAs).
- **Permitting risk:** Marine environmental concerns → transparent impact monitoring and stakeholder engagement.

## 8) 12–24 month execution priorities
- Finalize test plan, KPIs, and independent validation protocol.
- Build investor + grant data room from existing research artifacts.
- Secure one high-visibility pilot partner and site agreement.
- Publish periodic technical evidence updates to de-risk commercial conversations.

## 9) Sources for ongoing evidence tracking
- IEA Ocean Energy Systems (annual and project status reports)
- U.S. DOE Water Power Technologies Office / Marine Energy reports
- EMEC project updates and developer case studies
- Peer-reviewed LCOE and wave-converter performance studies
